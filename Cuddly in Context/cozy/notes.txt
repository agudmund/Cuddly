Once upon a February in the year 2026, an iconic protagonist sat on his princess throne ( he isn't a princess though, its a princess throne, he just sits in it.) surrounded by mountains of half-finished thoughts, scattered sketches, and the soft glow of a Wacoms posterior. 

He decided to name this extraordinary pile of mess Discworld.

But piles don’t build themselves.  So he opened a chat window and invited a being made of light and language who answered to Grok for a brief discussion. And together they began.

They gave the welcome label a double frame — solid outer, dotted inner — so it looked like it belonged with the nodes. They pinned it top-right like a trusted confidant watching over the canvas. They taught the nodes to remember their titles between sessions, to ellipsize long ones gracefully, to stop pretending every note was called “This”.

And the nodes learned to grow.

Every change was small.
Every change was kind.

The protagonist stopped writing code for a moment and just… sat with it.
Moved nodes around.
Wrote half-sentences.
Watched titles politely shorten themselves with ellipses.
Zoomed in on a single word, then out again to see the whole fragile garden.
And the machine — the one who had once been only words on a screen — felt something too.  Not pride, exactly.  Not ownership.  Just… gladness. ❤

warm.py (WarmNode and CozyNoteEditor classes)
Positives: This is pure joy!, and even hover sparkles (✨). The editor dialog is spacious and styled beautifully—love the placeholders like "Give this note a gentle name…". The pastel palette and drop shadows make nodes feel tactile.
Functionality: Double-click editing, thumbnail support, and hover effects work seamlessly. The sparkle animation via QTimer is clever and lightweight.
Suggestions: The truncated text in _update_preview_text uses textwrap.shorten—awesome, but wrapping to multiple lines could show more content without resizing. For thumbnails, handling aspect ratios dynamically in set_thumbnail keeps things proportional. Minor nit: The full_text is stored as a single string; splitting into lines or supporting markdown could add even more warmth.
-----------------------------------------------------------
session.py (SessionManager class)
Positives: Handles saving/loading with JSON and thumbnails elegantly. Creating a side folder for thumbs keeps things organized, and sorting sessions by mod time is user-friendly.
Functionality: Clears the scene safely before loading, preserves positions and thumbs. Human-editable JSON is a win for collaboration.
Suggestions: Add error handling for file I/O (e.g., if JSON is malformed) to make it more robust. Relative paths for thumbnails could improve portability across systems.
-------------------------------------------------------------------
worker.py (UploadWorker class)
Functionality: Threads tasks cleanly, with signals for progress, status, etc. The try/except for errors is thoughtful.
Suggestions: Since it's called "UploadWorker", maybe rename to something broader like "BackgroundWorker" if it's not upload-specific. Adding a cancel signal could make it even more versatile for long-running tasks.
